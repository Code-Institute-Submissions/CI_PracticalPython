By now, you examine your surrounding with a methodical eye. You observe the kitchen area where you currently stand. There is a narrow counter top under the windows and the dining room server, nothing out of the ordinary. You wonder how bothersome it was to take care of the dishes a century ago. You certainly are fond of the amenities offered by modern plumbing.

Your eyes pick up an old clock on the wall closest to the dining table. Beside being a gardener, you are fond of antiques and this pendulum clock charms you immediately. It is a well-preserved timepiece from the Victorian Era. However, as you get closer to it, it does not seem to be ticking. You know that clocks have a steady 'tick-tock' sounds not unlike a metronome when they are working properly.

As you stand next to the clock, you take note that across the room above the dining room server, there is a wedding portrait. In fact, it is so imposing that you wonder how it did not capture your attention when you came in.

What do you do next?

As you like tinkering with broken things, you know from experience that, if a pendulum clock has stopping, it could be due to its position. You think the clock is not quite level, so you poke at it with enthusiasm. Once satisfied that the clock is no longer leaning to the left or right, or at an angle, you expect the pendulum to start swinging again and wait to see if the metronome-like ticking is triggered.

  • Even if the clock is straightened, the pendulum does not seem to move. Confused, you open the clock's pendulum door to examine its innards. You gasp! There is a sheet wedged against the pendulum.

    As you unfold it, you see the sheet of paper is referring to some kind of maths problem (you grimace). There are handwritten notes and question marks and it looks like the note was ripped off a newspaper.

You have a close look at the wedding picture. The lady on the left has a beautiful white dress and the man looks a bit stiff in his fancy outfit. The picture has aged well and is enclosed in an elaborated wooden frame. The frame is bulky and is hung securely on the wall.
