Introduction

"Thanks again, dear {{ username }}!"

You see your neighbour waving from the street. You just helped her find her son's rattle under a pile of twigs.

In the neighbourhood, you are known for your inquisitive mind and problem-solving skills. You have uncovered more local mysteries than you can count (maths was never your forte anyways). You are not a professional, but you could be, if you worked up the courage to apply for a permit. You know investigating is not a glamorous endeavour. Your trusty {{ color }} umbrella in hand, you have braved spittle and deluge, dark basements and smelly back alleys whenever called for help. Really, it would be right in calling you a {{ mystical }} among civilians, such is your talent and dedication to puzzles. But to switch career from a gardener to a proper gumshoe...

One sunny day, while you are artfully pruning a shrub in front of your house, you are approached by an elderly couple, friends from your parents. They have heard of your prowess and ask you for help regarding the most unusual of situations.

They have inherited a little cottage in the countryside from relatives. Beside being a little spooky and dusty, it is quite charming and the couple wishes to use it as a summer house.

Recently, the couple entered the cottage to tidy it up. The previous residents have left furniture and decorations intact. It looked like a scene straight out of the early 20th century. As they walked in, their big dog Montague bumped into the mantelpiece and made a vase fall down. Within its debris, there was a note:

I left you a surprise, my love, to find when you miss me. My obligations won't keep me away from you too long, I dare hope. You will find a treasure where:
  • Time stops for our battles of wit.
  • White clad or not, you are a queen.
  • You inspire my every action.
  • I move brazenly towards you.
  • But you know how to stop my foolish plans.
  • I kneel in front of your beauty.
  • And carry the image of you with me.

You decide to help them with the riddle and tag along to look at the house.
