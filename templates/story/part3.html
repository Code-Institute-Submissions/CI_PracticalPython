3.

Happy with your observations so far, you walk to the side of the house closest to the kitchen stove. The only bed of the house is isolated not only by a curtain, but by two tall chests of drawers.The curtain is heavy with dust and you make the mistake of opening it brusquely.

Against the northern wall are two bookshelves crammed with books and papers.Three annotated picture frames hang over the bookshelves. A globe and an atlas are stored side by side on the bookshelf closest to the kitchen stove. The atlas pages are open on the American continent section. The other bookshelf contains newspapers piled up neatly alongside various tomes. They seem particularly worn.

Within hands' reach of the bookshelves, two comfortable-looking chairs show a lot of wear and tear. They are closely positioned to the low table. There are tracks on the floor showing the table and chairs were moved often.

Coughing heavily and cursing your impatience, you look under the bed frame and spot a wooden chest. There are also some hat boxes and plenty of dust bunnies rolling about.

  • A bouquet of dried up red roses is laid on a carefully folded wedding dress. Its white lace has been yellowed over time. Under the dress, there is a pair of gloves and some fancy shoes. Being a down-to-earth, unprivileged gardener, you do not know much about fashion, but you think this was a very respectable garment for a wedding a century ago.

The picture frames are identified as follows: Vera Menchik Club; My Queen; Battle in Black and White. You recognise that the two people sitting in the last picture are the same as the ones on the wedding portrait. More accurately, the picture seems to have been taken in this very room, and you see the low table by the kitchen stove matches the picture as well.

Carefully in case the chair's cushions are filled with dust, you sit down by the stove. You quickly identify the pattern on the low table in front of you. Of course! It is a chessboard table, with drawers where to store its pieces on each side. You admit to know very little of chess, but you recognize the basic pieces and you know how to set the board.

  • The board is already set in this case. However, there is a piece missing on the white player' side -- a knight. The white queen is occupying its spot.

You kneel by the bookshelf to review the available titles. There are various chess-related books and a few hourglasses. There are copies of the British Chess Magazine and an article clipping from the New York Times regarding the Carlsbad 1929 chess tournament. There are numerous articles piled up captioning interviews of famous chessmasters.

There is also a white knight sitting atop a newspaper clipping. The clipping has been scribbled over.

I left you a surprise, my love, to find when you miss me. My obligations won't keep me away from you too long, I dare hope. You will find a treasure where:
  • Time stops for our battles of wit.
  • White clad or not, you are a queen.
  • You inspire my every action.
  • I move brazenly towards you.
  • But you know how to stop my foolish plans.
  • I kneel in front of your beauty.
  • And carry the image of you with me.
