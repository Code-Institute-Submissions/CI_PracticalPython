You approach the house, led by the couple. They have told you on the way this house used to be owned by the woman's great-grand-parents. She presumes the riddle was left by the husband before he left on a trip abroad... from which he never returned.

You come in by the front door, which is quite low. Surprisingly, the house has electric lighting, so a flick of the hand is that is needed to illuminate the main room, even if it is a bit dimly.

From the door, you can see the house's only room is divided three unofficial areas. The first one would be the sitting area, close to the stove, where is a library along with a few couches and a small table. The second one would be the kitchen area, closest to the entrance's wall, where a small counter space is set under a few small windows. A dining table with two chairs sits between the areas. The last area you notice, farthest from the door, is the sleeping area, partially hidden behind a curtain and a chest of drawers. You understand that the privvy is adjacent to the house, considering its small size.
