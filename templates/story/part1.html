You approach the house, led by the McMillans. They have told you on the way the cottage was home to Mrs. McMillan's great-grandparents close to a century ago. She presumes the riddle was left by her great-grandfather, a journalist.

Apparently, he would have received an assignment requiring his presence in America, for which he left Europe and never returned. His wife would have followed him not too long after.

While the McMillans wait outside with their enthusiastic dog, you step into the house through the front door, which is quite low. To your surprise, the house has electric lighting, so a flick of the hand is all that is needed to illuminate the room. The light is still a bit dim.

You take in the cottage's antique charm. Its roof's wooden beams are exposed and are higher than you would have thought. The tiny windows are south-facing and allow for some natural light to shine in. From your position by the entrance, you can see the only room of the house is divided in three sections.

  • A kitchen area, closest to the entrance's wall, is set under a few small windows with a narrow counter space. The dining table and its two chairs sit between the sections.
  • A sitting area at the back of the room surrounds the kitchen stove and consists of bookshelves with a few couches and a low table.
  • Alongside it, farthest from the door is the sleeping area, partially hidden behind a curtain and a chest of drawers.

You assume that the privy is adjacent to the house considering the room's small size.

The dining table is covered by a tablecloth with an odd pattern. There is a slight bump under the cloth.

  • There is a bundle of sewing patterns. You can see one of them is meant for a wedding dress. There is an handwritten note on it: 'must be red, not white'.

You are impressed by the well-organised room in front of you. Clearly, the inhabitants had set up their sleeping and rest areas as to benefit from the heat of the stove. Everything within easy reach of the front door is related to the kitchen, and to the storage of goods and houseware. All items are arranged impeccably under a layer of dust.

I left you a surprise, my love, to find when you miss me. My obligations won't keep me away from you too long, I dare hope. You will find a treasure where:
  • Time stops for our battles of wit.
  • White clad or not, you are a queen.
  • You inspire my every action.
  • I move brazenly towards you.
  • But you know how to stop my foolish plans.
  • I kneel in front of your beauty.
  • And carry the image of you with me.
